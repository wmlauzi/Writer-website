About Maren

Seven years of finding the right words.

I'm Maren Sollis — a writer, essayist, and brand voice specialist based in Edinburgh, Scotland. My work sits at the intersection of culture, language, and the quietly profound. I believe the most important ideas are often the ones that feel too small to write about.

My essays have appeared in The Atlantic, Aeon, Delayed Gratification, The Believer, and various literary journals I'm still proud to name-drop at dinner. Alongside the writing, I help founders, executives, and brands find language that is genuinely theirs.

Before I became a full-time writer, I studied English Literature at the University of St Andrews and spent two restless years as a sub-editor at a regional newspaper — which taught me everything about deadlines and nothing about how to write a lede.

A Brief History
2018

Quit the day job

Left sub-editing to write full-time. Terrifying. Worth it. First essay published in Aeon three months later.

2019

Started the newsletter

The Marginal Note launched with 47 subscribers (mostly my mum's book club). Now 28,000 readers every other Sunday.

2021

First commission from The Atlantic

"The Lost Art of Boredom" ran as a cover essay. Still my most-read piece, which says something interesting about the times.

2022

Brand work begins

Started taking on voice and copy projects for brands. Discovered that writing for companies and writing essays use completely different muscles.

2024

140th published essay

Also: moved to a flat with a proper writing room. A desk by the window. A kettle within reach. Everything I ever wanted.
